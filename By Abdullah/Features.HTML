Car Features

Performance

Back to main page
  • Powerful Engines: Ferrari engines are renowned for their performance. Most models feature naturally aspirated V8 or V12 engines, with some newer models incorporating hybrid systems for an additional boost in power. For example.
  • The Ferrari SF90 Stradale has a V8 hybrid engine that delivers over 1,000 horsepower.
  • The Ferrari 812 Superfast is powered by a naturally aspirated V12 engine.
  • Acceleration and Speed: Ferrari cars are built for extreme performance. The F8 Tributo can go from 0 to 60 mph in under 3 seconds, and the SF90 Stradale has a top speed of 211 mph (340 km/h).

Handling and Driving Experience

  • Ferrari Dynamic Enhancer (FDE): This system enhances the car’s stability and handling, especially during high-speed cornering, making the car incredibly agile on tight and challenging roads or tracks.
  • Magnetic Ride Control: Most modern Ferraris use adaptive suspension systems with magnetic dampers to offer a smooth ride while maintaining precise handling in various driving conditions.
  • Dual-Clutch Transmission: Ferrari cars typically come with a dual-clutch gearbox, which provides incredibly fast gear shifts, improving both acceleration and driving enjoyment.
  • Electronic Differential (E-Diff): This system optimizes torque distribution between the rear wheels for improved cornering and stability.

Exterior Design

  • Aerodynamic Styling: Ferrari cars are designed with sleek, flowing lines that are not just about looks but also serve to enhance aerodynamic performance. The rear diffusers, active flaps, and other elements help with downforce and reduce drag.
  • Carbon Fiber Components: Many Ferrari models feature carbon fiber body panels and interior components for both weight reduction and added strength.
  • Signature Headlights: Ferrari’s distinctive, aggressive headlights often feature LED technology, adding to the modern, high-tech look.

Interior and Comfort

  • Luxury Materials: Inside, Ferrari uses the finest materials, including leather, Alcantara, carbon fiber, and premium stitching to create a luxurious yet driver-focused cabin.
  • Ergonomic Seats: Ferrari seats are designed for comfort as well as support, with many models offering adjustable, bucket-style racing seats. Some seats even have custom upholstery options to match the buyer’s personal preferences.
  • Driver-Focused Cockpit: The layout of the controls and displays is optimized for the driver. The steering wheel typically incorporates various driving modes and functions (such as launch control, gear shifting, and more) for easy access.

Technology and Infotainment

  • Advanced Infotainment System: Ferrari’s infotainment systems are designed for simplicity and performance, often featuring touchscreen displays that integrate seamlessly with mobile devices. Apple CarPlay and Android Auto are often available for easy smartphone connectivity.
  • Telematics and Performance Tracking: Ferrari cars come with telemetry systems that allow drivers to track their driving performance, such as lap times, G-force, and engine data.
  • Driver Assistance Systems: Ferrari has incorporated advanced features like adaptive cruise control, parking sensors, and reverse cameras, but they’re generally designed to enhance the driving experience, not take away from it.

Sound and Exhaust

  • Distinctive Engine Sound: One of the signature features of a Ferrari is the incredible sound of its engine. The exhaust system is designed to produce an unmistakable, aggressive, high-pitched note, especially at higher revs.
  • Active Exhaust System: Some models, like the Ferrari 488 GTB, come with an active exhaust system that adjusts the exhaust tone based on driving conditions and rev levels.

Safety and Stability

  • Carbon Ceramic Brakes: Ferrari’s carbon-ceramic brakes are extremely powerful, providing superior braking performance even at high speeds and reducing the likelihood of brake fade during track use.
  • Traction Control: Advanced traction control systems help maintain stability by modulating power to the wheels, especially in slippery or low-traction conditions.
  • Safety Cell and Crumple Zones: Ferrari builds its cars with a rigid safety cell and energy-absorbing crumple zones to help protect passengers in the event of a crash, along with the latest airbag and safety belt systems.

Customization Options

  • Tailored Designs: Ferrari offers extensive customization options through its Tailor Made program, where buyers can choose everything from paint colors, interior finishes, seat styles, and even stitching patterns to create a unique car.
  • Limited Editions: Ferrari often releases limited edition models with exclusive features or finishes, adding to the exclusivity and allure of the brand.

Hybrid Technology (for newer models)

  • Hybrid Powertrains: The latest models like the SF90 Stradale include a hybrid system that combines electric motors with a traditional V8 engine. This provides greater fuel efficiency and performance without sacrificing power.
  • Regenerative Braking: The hybrid systems also use regenerative braking, which captures energy during braking to recharge the battery, making the car more efficient.
